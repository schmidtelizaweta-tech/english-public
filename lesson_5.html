Lesson 5

Cyber Scam Awareness

Focus on online safety, scam patterns, trust, and clear decisions under pressure.

Level: B1–B2 Time: 60 minutes Topic: cyber scams, warning signs, and safe responses
Warm-up — Vocabulary Review 10 min

Click each card to reveal the meaning and a short example.

Select a word to reveal its meaning.
Discussion Questions 30 min

Choose one question at a time and speak for about one minute. Try to add examples, reasons, and a clear opinion.

Part A — Awareness

  1. What are the most common warning signs of a scam?
  2. How can people protect themselves from online fraud?
  3. Why do some people fall for scams even when they seem obvious?
  4. What is the difference between a risky message and a normal one?

Part B — Personal perspective

  1. Have you ever received a suspicious message or call?
  2. What would you do if someone asked for your bank details immediately?
  3. Do you think people trust online offers too quickly?
  4. How important is it to verify a company before sharing any information?

Extension: Compare your answer with a partner and explain your reasoning.

Conflict Situations 20 min

Read each scenario, discuss what the person should do, and then reveal a possible response.

1. Urgent payment

A message says your bank account will be closed unless you confirm your details today. The sender sounds calm, but the message creates pressure.

A strong reply is: “I will not share personal details by message. I will contact the bank directly through the official number.”

2. Fake investment offer

A caller promises very high returns in just a few days and says the opportunity is available only for a short time.

A strong reply is: “This sounds unrealistic. I need time to verify the company before I make any decision.”

3. Prize message

A person receives an email saying they have won a prize, but the link asks for a passport number and an address.

A strong reply is: “I would never share sensitive information through an unknown link. I would ignore the message and report it.”

4. Pressure from a friend

A friend sends a message saying, “Please send me the code from your verification app. I need it now.”

A strong reply is: “I cannot share that code. It is private and could put my account at risk.”
