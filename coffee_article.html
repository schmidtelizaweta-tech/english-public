Coffee Culture

Read the article, learn the most useful expressions, and prepare a short speaking response. Aim for quality, not speed.

Coffee is more than a drink for many people. In cities around the world, it has become a daily ritual, a social habit, and sometimes even a part of someone’s identity. For some, the morning begins with a strong cup of coffee and a few quiet minutes before the day gets busy. For others, the best part of the day is the moment they brew a fresh cup and sit down with a notebook or a friend.

In many cafés, people do more than drink coffee. They sip slowly, look out of the window, and drift into a conversation that starts with a simple question about the week. Coffee can also be a quick caffeine fix on a rushed morning, but for many people it is a ritual of comfort, something they return to when life feels stressful.

However, the coffee world is not only about comfort. Some people get hooked on coffee and feel they cannot start the day without it. Others try to cut back on caffeine because they want better sleep or lower anxiety. The conversation around coffee is changing too. More people now care about where the beans come from, how they are roasted, and whether the coffee is fair trade.
